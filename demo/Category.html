Category

There are various ways of classifying Chinese teas, the accepted one being that they can be divided into six main categories according to the method of production and the degree of oxidation (fermentation) of the tea polyphenols: green tea (unfermented), white tea (slightly fermented), yellow tea (lightly fermented), green tea (also called oolong tea, semi-fermented), black tea (post-fermented) and black tea (fully fermented). The appearance changes gradually from green to yellow-green, yellow, green-brown, dark green and black, and the tea broth also changes gradually from green to yellow-green, yellow, green-brown and reddish-brown.
